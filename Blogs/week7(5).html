Website inspiration

By Sisanda Gabela.

Date published: 31 March 2025

This week, I started exploring potential websites for my UI/UX analysis essay, and it definitely opened my eyes to how differently design functions across different industries.

I eventually focused on sorbet.co.za for my main analysis. It stood out because of its clean visuals, targeted branding, and user experience that really reflects its identity as a self-care and beauty brand. There were some clear UX gaps I wanted to explore, especially around accessibility and mobile responsiveness, so it made a strong case for my final critical analysis.

Before choosing it as my final analysis though, I also looked at:

netflorist.co.za: I liked how bright and bold it felt, but the UI felt cluttered at times, and I noticed the navigation wasn’t always intuitive. Some pages also felt overloaded with content.

zeitzmocaa.museum: Super sleek and minimalist. Visually, it was stunning, but maybe a bit too minimal—especially when it came to guiding user interaction or ensuring accessibility for different users.

Each of these websites had unique strengths and weaknesses, but in the end, Sorbet gave me the widest areas to explore both within aesthetic design and ethical UX considerations.
